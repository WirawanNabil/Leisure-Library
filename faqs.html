FREQUENTLY ASK QUESTIONS

  • How do I get a library card?
     To obtain a library card, visit the library's circulation desk with a valid ID and proof of address. The staff will guide you through the application process.


  • What resources and services does the library offer?
     The library provides a variety of resources, including books, e-books, audiobooks, digital databases, and services such as workshops, events, and reference assistance.


  • How can I renew or return borrowed materials?
     You can renew materials online through your library account or return them to the library's drop-off area. Renewal options and return instructions are available on the library's website.


  • Is Wi-Fi available in the library?
     Yes, the library offers free Wi-Fi for patrons. You can connect to the library's network using your device within the library premises.


  • Are there meeting rooms or study spaces available for reservation?
     Yes, the library has meeting rooms and study spaces available for reservation. Please check with the library staff for availability and reservation details.


  • How can I access the library's digital resources from home?
     You can access the library's digital resources remotely by logging in to your library account on the official website. Follow the provided instructions for accessing e-books, databases, and other digital materials.


  • How can I donate books or materials to the library?
     If you wish to donate books or materials to the library, inquire at the circulation desk. The staff will provide information on the library's donation policies and guidelines.


  • Is there a limit on the number of items I can borrow at one time?
     The library may have a borrowing limit. Check with the circulation desk or refer to the library's policies on the maximum number of items you can borrow at a time.
